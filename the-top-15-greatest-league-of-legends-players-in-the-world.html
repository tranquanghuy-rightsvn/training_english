The Top 15 Greatest League of Legends Players in the World

  • 19:57 06/08/2024
League of Legends (LoL), developed by Riot Games, has become one of the most popular and competitive esports globally. Over the years, many players have risen to prominence through their skills, strategies, and contributions to the game. Here, we celebrate the top 15 League of Legends players in the world, highlighting their achievements, playstyles, and impact on the esports community.

1. Faker (Lee Sang-hyeok)

Known as the "Unkillable Demon King," Faker is widely regarded as the greatest League of Legends player of all time. Playing for T1, his incredible mechanical skills, strategic understanding, and consistency have earned him three World Championship titles (2013, 2015, 2016). Faker's adaptability across multiple metas and champions makes him a legendary figure in the LoL community. His ability to make clutch plays under pressure, along with his deep understanding of the game's intricacies, has kept him at the top of his game for years. Faker's influence extends beyond the game, as he is a role model for many aspiring players worldwide.



2. Uzi (Jian Zi-Hao)

Uzi, a former ADC for Royal Never Give Up (RNG), is renowned for his aggressive playstyle and exceptional mechanics. Despite never winning a World Championship, his influence and dominance in the bot lane have left a lasting legacy. Uzi's dedication and passion for the game have inspired countless fans and aspiring players. His laning phase prowess and ability to carry games from behind have set him apart from other ADCs. Uzi's career has been marked by numerous individual accolades, and his impact on the Chinese LoL scene is immeasurable. His retirement in 2020 was a significant moment for the community, highlighting his lasting impact on the game.

3. Caps (Rasmus Winther)

Caps, a mid-laner for G2 Esports, has consistently demonstrated his prowess on the international stage. Known for his creative plays and champion pool, Caps has reached the World Championship finals twice (2018, 2019) and has been a pivotal figure in the European LoL scene. His ability to carry games and adapt to different roles showcases his versatility. Caps' aggressive playstyle and willingness to take risks often result in spectacular plays that can turn the tide of a match. His journey from Fnatic to G2 Esports marked a new era in his career, where he continued to showcase his exceptional skills and game sense.

4. TheShy (Kang Seung-lok)

TheShy, a top laner for Invictus Gaming (IG), is celebrated for his aggressive and dominant laning phase. His performance was instrumental in IG's victory at the 2018 World Championship. TheShy's mechanical skill and innovative champion picks have set him apart as one of the best top laners in the world. His ability to outplay opponents in one-on-one situations and his fearless approach to the game have made him a fan favorite. TheShy's impact extends beyond his individual skill, as his presence in the top lane often dictates the flow of the game for his team.

5. Perkz (Luka Perković)

Perkz, known for his flexibility in playing both mid and ADC roles, has been a cornerstone of G2 Esports' success. His leadership and playmaking ability have helped G2 secure multiple LEC titles and a runner-up finish at the 2019 World Championship. Perkz's strategic mind and adaptability are key to his success. His decision to switch roles from mid-lane to ADC and then back to mid-lane showcased his versatility and team-first mentality. Perkz's impact on the European LoL scene is profound, and his charismatic personality has made him a beloved figure in the community.

6. Doinb (Kim Tae-sang)

Doinb, a mid-laner for FunPlus Phoenix (FPX), is known for his unconventional champion picks and supportive playstyle. Leading FPX to a World Championship title in 2019, Doinb's innovative approach and in-game leadership have made him a standout player in the LoL community. His ability to bring unique champions into the mid-lane and his exceptional roaming skills have disrupted traditional mid-lane playstyles. Doinb's cheerful personality and strong work ethic have endeared him to fans and teammates alike, making him a respected figure in the competitive scene.



7. Smeb (Song Kyung-ho)

Smeb, a top laner formerly of KT Rolster and ROX Tigers, is known for his consistent performance and versatility. His mastery of a wide range of champions and ability to impact games from the top lane have earned him recognition as one of the best in his role. Smeb's presence in the Korean LoL scene has been significant over the years. His ability to play both carry and tank champions with equal proficiency has made him a formidable opponent. Smeb's leadership and calm demeanor under pressure have been crucial to his teams' successes, and his influence extends beyond his individual performance.

8. Doublelift (Yiliang Peng)

Doublelift, a former ADC for teams like TSM and Team Liquid, is one of North America's most successful and popular players. Known for his aggressive playstyle and trash-talking, Doublelift has won multiple LCS titles and has been a key figure in promoting LoL in North America. His career has been marked by resilience and a drive to succeed. Doublelift's ability to dominate the laning phase and transition into a late-game carry role has been a hallmark of his play. His charismatic personality and candid interviews have made him a beloved and sometimes controversial figure in the LoL community.

9. Ruler (Park Jae-hyuk)

Ruler, an ADC for Gen.G, rose to fame with his performance at the 2017 World Championship, where he helped Samsung Galaxy secure the title. Known for his precise positioning and team fighting skills, Ruler has continued to be a dominant force in the bot lane, consistently performing at a high level in the LCK. His ability to remain calm and collected during intense moments has been crucial to his success. Ruler's consistent performance and dedication to improvement have made him a key player in the Korean LoL scene, and his impact on the international stage is undeniable.

10. Clearlove (Ming Kai)

Clearlove, a jungler for EDward Gaming (EDG), is a veteran of the Chinese LoL scene. Known for his exceptional game sense and control over objectives, Clearlove has led EDG to multiple LPL titles and an MSI championship. His strategic mind and experience make him one of the most respected junglers in the game. Clearlove's ability to coordinate with his teammates and execute precise ganks has been a significant factor in his team's successes. His longevity in the competitive scene and his contributions to the growth of LoL in China have cemented his legacy as a legendary player.

11. Bjergsen (Søren Bjerg)

Bjergsen, a mid-laner and coach for TSM, has been a dominant force in the North American scene for years. Known for his consistent performance and leadership, Bjergsen has won numerous LCS titles and has been a key figure in TSM's success. His ability to adapt to different metas and champions has kept him at the top of his game. Bjergsen's calm and analytical approach to the game, combined with his mechanical skill, has made him a pillar of the NA LoL scene. His transition to coaching reflects his deep understanding of the game and his commitment to fostering new talent.



12. JackeyLove (Yu Wen-Bo)

JackeyLove, an ADC for Top Esports, is known for his aggressive and confident playstyle. Winning the World Championship with Invictus Gaming in 2018, JackeyLove has continued to be a dominant force in the LPL. His mechanics and ability to carry games make him one of the top ADCs in the world. JackeyLove's fearless approach to the game and his ability to perform under pressure have earned him a reputation as a clutch player. His contributions to his teams' successes and his impact on the Chinese LoL scene are significant, and his career continues to flourish.

13. Chovy (Jeong Ji-hoon)

Chovy, a mid-laner for Hanwha Life Esports, is known for his incredible laning phase and mechanical prowess. Despite not having many international titles, Chovy's individual skill and consistency have earned him recognition as one of the best mid-laners in the world. His potential for growth and impact on the game is immense. Chovy's ability to dominate his lane opponents and his exceptional decision-making have made him a standout player in the LCK. His calm and composed demeanor in high-stakes matches reflects his maturity and dedication to mastering his craft.

14. Mata (Cho Se-hyeong)

Mata, a former support player and coach, is known for his exceptional game knowledge and vision control. Winning the World Championship with Samsung White in 2014, Mata's strategic mind and leadership have had a lasting impact on the game. His contributions to the support role and the broader strategic play of teams are widely respected. Mata's ability to control the map and provide crucial information to his team has been a cornerstone of his playstyle. His transition to coaching highlights his deep understanding of the game and his desire to continue contributing to the LoL community.

15. Ambition (Kang Chan-yong)

Ambition, a former jungler for Samsung Galaxy, is known for his veteran presence and leadership. Initially starting his career as a mid-laner, Ambition transitioned to the jungle role, demonstrating his adaptability and strategic mind. His most notable achievement came in 2017 when he led Samsung Galaxy to a World Championship title, defeating the heavily favored SK Telecom T1. Ambition's consistent and steady playstyle, coupled with his precise decision-making and control over objectives, earned him immense respect in the LoL community. Even after retiring, he continues to contribute as a content creator and analyst, leaving a lasting legacy in the esports scene.



Conclusion

These top 15 League of Legends players have not only demonstrated exceptional skill and dedication but have also significantly influenced the game's development and the esports scene. Their legacies continue to inspire new generations of players and fans, cementing their places as legends in the world of League of Legends.
