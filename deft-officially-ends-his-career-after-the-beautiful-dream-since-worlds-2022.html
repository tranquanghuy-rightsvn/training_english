Deft Officially Ends His Career After the Beautiful Dream Since Worlds 2022

  • 18:54 16/09/2024
As soon as the final match of the LCK Regional Qualifier officially ended, Deft also left behind the beautiful dream he had since Worlds 2022. At Worlds 2022, DRX embarked on what was described as an "unbelievable" journey, earning them much praise under the name of the "Blue Dragons." Particularly, the most talked-about player in this tournament was none other than Deft—the oldest ADC to ever win a Worlds championship. Riot Games even released a song titled "GODS" dedicated to him as a tribute to his tumultuous career before Worlds 2023. 



However, not everyone knows that Deft had hinted several times that after 2024, regardless of KT Rolster's performance, he would retire to fulfill his military service. Yet, in the final match of his career, Deft had a lackluster performance and hardly made any impact on the game.

Throughout all five games, Deft was KT's lowest-rated player. One fan comment that touched many hearts read:

"Everything should have ended after Worlds 2022, but you were still in that dream. Now the dream is over, and it's time to say goodbye, Deft."



Since Worlds 2022, Deft has had some performances reminiscent of his peak, but for a while now, he has no longer been the ADC who can carry when given enough resources like Aiming or Peyz. Nor can he create the extraordinary plays that players like Viper or Gumayusi have.

In the final game of the series between T1 and KT, there was a moment that struck a chord with viewers—a poignant image of Faker chasing Deft, evoking memories of their previous encounters. Although Deft will not be returning to Worlds, he has likely completed his mission, achieving all the glory that a League of Legends player could ever dream of.
